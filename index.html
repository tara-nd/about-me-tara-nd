What is quantum physics, and how does it work? Simply said, physics is the best description we have of the nature of the particles that make up matter and the forces with which they interact, and it explains how everything works.

Quantum physics explains how atoms work, as well as how chemistry and biology work. At some level, you, myself, and the gatepost are all dancing to the quantum song. Quantum physics is required to explain how electrons move through a computer chip, how photons of light are converted to electrical current in a solar panel or amplify themselves in a laser, or even how the sun continues to burn.

Niels Bohr, Werner Heisenberg, Erwin Schr√∂dinger and others are the so-called "Founding Fathers" of quantum mechanics.

Quantum mechanics is the mathematical framework that underpins it all(biology,chemistry,physics) Quantum mechanics must be integrated with other aspects of physics to generate quantum field theories, which explain how things behave in the actual world. The most famous of these is Albert Einstein's special theory of relativity, which explains what happens when things move very quickly.

Three different quantum field theories address three of the four fundamental forces that interact with matter: electromagnetism, which explains how atoms stay together; the strong nuclear force, which explains the stability of the nucleus at the heart of the atom; and the weak nuclear force, which explains why some atoms decay radioactively.
